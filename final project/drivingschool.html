What to expect at driving school

After you obtain your commercial learners permit you can now go to a driving school
Highly recommended that you go online and look at schools in your area. Check reviews and call them to see if they're the right fit for you since you're going to be spending thousands of dollars
It's important to know that schools sell classes in packages, this means that they will sell a certain number of classes as a package deal and if you run out of classes then you will need to buy more
My school for example offered unlimited number of classes so you wouldn't have to worry about that, but it cost more money
Once at school the first thing that they will go over is whats called a pre tip inspection
A pre trip inspection includes the inspection of the interior, windows, windshield, the engine compartment, steering axles, suspension, wheels, break pads, rims, hub axles seals, air lines connection, fifth wheel, coupling trailer lights, landing gear, and the rear doors of the trailer
Yes you will need to memorize everything but your school will guide you in what to look at and what to say
A tip for memorizing is if its metal it's not cracked or broken, and if its plastic it's not worn, cracked, or loose
Your school will also begin training you on how to operate a class a vehicle
They will start out with making you reverse in a straight line
As much as it sounds easy you must remember that all the rules of driving a car are thrown out the window
When backing a trailer you must always remember that which ever way you want your trailer to go you must turn to the oppostie direction, so if you want to get the trailer to go the right you must turn your wheel to the left and vice versa
Your school will also train you on how to parallel park a truck and trailer as well as how to do an alley dock which is the most common way to back a truck
The school that you choose will also take you out on the road so you can learn how to make turns in the real world
*Always remember to open up wide when making a turn
When you're finally ready for a road test your school will schedule you for a date
Your road test will consist of three parts The first portion is the pre trip portion where you will have to show the instructor at the DMV that you know what components of the truck must be inspected as well as being knowledgeable about the components
*If you don't pass this portion you cannot move onto driving
The next portion will be the skills test portion
This is where you will demonstrate that you can reverse in a straight line, alley dock, offset, and parallel park the truck and trailer
If you pass you will you go out on the road with the instructor
They have pre planned routes and they will ask you to make left turns, right turns, and also remember you must obey all traffic laws
After completing all three portions you're done with your road test and can get your license
